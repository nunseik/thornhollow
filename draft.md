# The Wizard of Thornhollow
## A Complete D&D-Style Adventure

---

# Prologue

*The torchlight flickered against the damp stone walls of the cell. Theron Ashborne had been here three days now—or was it four? Time moved strangely in the Witch of Thornhollow's domain.*

He sat cross-legged on the cold floor, his spellbook confiscated, his component pouch taken. But they hadn't found the small piece of copper wire sewn into his collar. And more importantly, they couldn't take what was in his mind.

Through the iron bars, he could see:

**The sleeping guard** — a hulking figure slumped against the far wall, a ring of keys glinting at his belt. His snoring echoes through the corridor.

**The crack in the mortar** — behind his straw pallet, he'd noticed loose stones. With effort, he might widen the gap... but to where?

**The rat** — it watched him with unusual intelligence. He'd heard whispers that the witch kept familiars everywhere. Or perhaps it was just a rat.

From somewhere above, he heard the witch's voice raised in incantation. She was preparing something. The air tasted of ozone and old blood.

---

# Chapter 1: The Escape

## Casting Message

With practiced subtlety, he completed the casting without drawing the guard's attention. The magic took hold. He felt the invisible thread of the Message cantrip extend outward—but here was the problem: Message let him whisper to a creature he could see, and they could whisper back. It didn't move objects.

*However.*

His mind worked quickly. He decided to whisper to the rat instead—if it *was* a familiar, perhaps he could bargain. If it was just a rat... well, he'd wasted a cantrip.

---

## The Familiar's Bargain

*Theron turned his gaze to the rat. Its dark eyes glittered in the torchlight—watching, waiting. He extended the Message cantrip toward it, the copper wire warm between his fingers.*

"Little one," *he whispered through the arcane thread,* "I know what you are. Let's talk."

*A pause. The rat's whiskers twitched.*

Then—a voice in his mind. Dry, amused, ancient.

"Clever little mage. Most prisoners just throw stones at me."

*It was a familiar. The witch's eyes and ears in the dungeon.*

Theron chose his words carefully, appealing to the creature's obvious intelligence and the hint of weariness he detected in its tone.

The rat scratched behind its ear, then sat up on its haunches with unsettling poise.

"I've served the Thornhollow witch for... oh, longer than you've been alive. But I'll admit, she's grown *tedious*. All blood rituals and monologuing. No appreciation for the simple pleasures—cheese, warmth, interesting conversation."

It fixed Theron with those knowing eyes.

"I won't betray her outright. But perhaps I could be... momentarily distracted. Fail to notice certain things. For a price."

The familiar tilted its head.

"When you escape—and you *will* escape, or you'll die, either way—I want you to take me with you. A new contract. A wizard who actually *talks* to me. Do we have a deal?"

Above, a thunderclap. The ritual intensified.

*Theron met the rat's gaze and nodded slowly.*

"Deal."

*The familiar's whiskers twitched—something like a smile, if rats could smile.*

"Wisely done, wizard. My name is Whisper. Remember it."

---

## The Keys

Whisper scurried toward the sleeping guard with purposeful silence. Theron watched as the tiny creature climbed the man's boot, his leg, his belt—and with surprisingly dexterous paws, worked at the keyring's clasp.

The keys slid free without a sound. Whisper dragged them across the flagstones, the metal barely clinking, and pushed them through the gap beneath Theron's cell bars.

"Hurry," the familiar hissed. "She's summoning something. When it arrives, she won't need you alive anymore—just your *blood*."

Theron grabbed the cold iron ring. Three keys: one blackened, one silver, one bone-white.

The lock on his cell was old and rusted.

*He chose the blackened key. Instinct—or perhaps the faint smell of sulfur that reminded him of his first evocation lessons. Fire undoes locks. Fire opens ways.*

He jammed it into the rusted mechanism and twisted.

**Click.**

*The cell door groaned open.*

"Good choice," Whisper chittered, scrambling up his leg to perch on his shoulder. "The silver one triggers an alarm. The bone key... you don't want to know what the bone key opens."

---

## The Illusion

Theron stepped into the corridor. The guard still slept—Whisper's doing, he suspected, more than natural exhaustion. To his left, a spiral staircase climbed toward the sound of that horrible tearing. To his right, a darker passage sloped downward, carrying the smell of earth and something older.

Then he heard it.

A voice from above. The witch—but *wrong*. Layered. As if something else was speaking *through* her.

**"THE BINDING IS COMPLETE. NOW... WHERE IS MY SACRIFICE?"**

Footsteps. Coming down the stairs.

Whisper's claws dug into his shoulder.

"Your spellbook is in her study—up. Your freedom is in the old tunnels—down. And your component pouch..." The rat paused. "She's *wearing* it. As a trophy."

*Survival over pride. A wise wizard knows when to flee.*

"Down it is," Whisper agreed. "I was hoping you'd say that."

Theron plunged into the descending passage, one hand trailing the damp stone wall, the darkness swallowing him whole. Behind him, the footsteps reached the dungeon level. A shriek of rage—the witch had found his empty cell.

**"FIND THE WIZARD. FIND HIM NOW."**

The tunnel twisted, branched, doubled back. Without Whisper's whispered directions—"left, left, right, straight, ignore that passage it leads to the oubliette"—he'd have been hopelessly lost.

The sounds of pursuit faded. The air grew colder, damper, then suddenly *fresher*.

He saw it: a pale sliver of moonlight.

The tunnel opened into a drainage grate overlooking a forest ravine. The ironwork was old, rusted. One solid kick might—

He threw his weight against it. The grate groaned but held. Footsteps echoed behind him now—distant, but growing closer.

"Again!" Whisper urged.

---

## Hiding from the Witch

*Theron pressed his back against the cold stone, mind racing through his mental spellbook. Without components, without his focus, his options were limited—but not gone.*

*He still had his mind. And some spells needed nothing more.*

"Whisper," he breathed, "stay perfectly still."

He closed his eyes and wove the incantation for **Minor Illusion**—no components required, just will and words. The magic responded, shaping shadow and silence into a false wall of crumbling stone exactly where he stood, blending seamlessly with the tunnel's ancient architecture.

He held his breath. Whisper became a statue on his shoulder.

Torchlight flickers around the bend. Two figures—no, three. The witch's servants: hollow-eyed thralls with jerking movements, sniffing the air like hounds. They pass the drainage grate. One stops *directly in front of his illusion*.

Its head tilts. Sniffs.

*Seconds stretch into eternity.*

Then it turns and shambles onward, following the others deeper into the tunnel network.

"They'll circle back," Whisper whispers. "We have minutes."

The grate still stands between he and freedom. Moonlight taunts he through the gaps.

---

## The Clever Escape

*A grin spread across his face. Why break down the door himself when his enemies can do it for him?*

"Oh, I *like* you," Whisper murmured, sensing his intent.

Theron focused his will, shaping another Minor Illusion just beyond the grate—a flickering image of himself, stumbling through the moonlit ravine, robes torn, looking back in terror. He added sound: ragged breathing, snapping twigs, a desperate voice calling out—

"This way! I see the river!"

The effect was perfect. His phantom-self scrambled up the ravine with convincing panic, its voice echoing back through the drainage tunnel.

He heard the thralls halt. Then—a horrible screech. They'd taken the bait.

The three servants came *thundering* back down the passage. Theron flattened himself against the wall, his stone-illusion still holding, as they barreled past. The lead thrall didn't slow down.

It *slammed* into the rusted grate with the full force of its unnatural momentum.

**CRASH.**

The ironwork tore free from the ancient mortar and tumbled into the ravine below, taking the thrall with it. The other two leapt through the gap without hesitation, pursuing his phantom into the forest.

The way was open.

Cold night air washed over his face. Stars. Freedom.

"Move," Whisper urged. "*Now*, before they realize."

*Theron slipped through the broken grate and into the wild darkness of Thornhollow Forest.*

---

# Chapter 2: The River

*Smart. The river masks scent, muffles sound, and eventually—civilization.*

Theron half-slid, half-scrambled down the ravine, loose stones skittering beneath his feet. The moonlight caught the water below: black and swift, cutting through the forest like a silver knife.

"Can you swim?" Whisper asked, claws tightening.

"Can *you*?"

"I'm a rat. We're *excellent* swimmers. It's the cold I'm worried about."

He hit the water and gasped—it was *freezing*, snowmelt from the northern peaks. But he found his footing on the rocky riverbed, the current tugging at his robes. He pushed off, letting the flow carry him downstream, keeping his head low. Whisper clung to his collar, nose just above the waterline, whiskers twitching with displeasure but no complaint.

Behind them, the thralls' cries grew distant. Confused. They were chasing his phantom deeper into the forest, away from the water.

---

## Rest and Recovery

The river bent. Through the thinning canopy, he spotted it: the old road, a pale ribbon of packed earth running parallel to the water. And beyond—distant lights. A village? A waystation?

Theron dragged himself onto the muddy bank, shivering, exhausted. Whisper shook himself dry with offended dignity.

"Well, wizard. We're alive." The familiar surveyed the road, then him. "What's the plan? Rest and recover? Or push on through the night?"

The witch wouldn't stop hunting. But he was cold, spent, and magically depleted.

*He decided to rest tonight and make his way to the village in the morning.*

"Whisper," he said through chattering teeth, already fumbling with the clasps of his sodden robes, "I need food. Anything."

The familiar fixed him with an unreadable look. "I'm your *familiar* now, not your servant."

A pause.

"...But I suppose if you die of hunger, our contract becomes rather meaningless."

He vanished into the undergrowth.

---

Theron found a hollow beneath an ancient oak—roots gnarled overhead like protective fingers, thick enough to block the wind and hide a small fire if he dared one. He didn't. Not tonight.

He stripped down and hung his robes over a low branch, wringing what water he could from the fabric. The night air bit at his skin, but he found dry leaves and moss to pile beneath the oak's shelter—not warm, but warmer than the river.

Passable. He wouldn't freeze. Probably.

---

Twenty minutes later, Whisper returns. He drops his offering at his feet with obvious pride:

Three acorns. A handful of wild blackberries, only slightly crushed. And—improbably—half a bread roll, tooth-marked but edible.

"Found a traveler's dropped satchel upstream," he explains. "The bread was *already* bitten. Mostly."

It's the best meal he'd ever tasted.

---

*He drifted into fitful sleep, curled beneath the oak, his familiar keeping watch with ancient, patient eyes.*

Morning would bring the road. The village. And decisions about what came next.

*The witch was still out there. She had his spellbook. His component pouch. And now, a grudge.*

---

**Dawn broke cold and grey.**

Theron woke stiff, hungry again, but alive. His robes were damp but wearable. Whisper sat on a root nearby, grooming his whiskers.

"The village is perhaps an hour's walk," he says. "I scouted ahead. It's small—tavern, smithy, chapel, a few farms. Nothing special." He pauses. "There's also a notice board outside the tavern. Someone's posted a bounty."

His eyes glitter.

"For a *witch*."

---

# Chapter 3: The Drowned Crow

*Theron pulled on his still-damp robes with a grimace and set off toward the village, Whisper nestled in his hood. The road was quiet—just birdsong and the distant lowing of cattle.*

An hour later, he crested a small hill and saw it: **Millhaven**. A humble cluster of thatched roofs and chimney smoke, exactly as Whisper had described. The tavern sat at the village's heart, a weathered sign creaking in the morning breeze.

*The Drowned Crow.*

Charming.

---

Theron pushed through the door. Warmth washed over him—a crackling hearth, the smell of bacon and fresh bread, the murmur of early morning conversation. A few heads turned: a farmer nursing an ale, two cloaked figures hunched over a map in the corner, a barmaid wiping down tables.

Behind the bar, a heavyset woman with kind eyes and flour-dusted arms looks he over.

"Rough night, traveler?"

"You could say that."

She grunts knowingly and slides a wooden menu across the bar.

---

**Breakfast at The Drowned Crow**

| Item | Price |
|------|-------|
| Bacon, eggs, and black bread | 5 copper |
| Porridge with honey | 2 copper |
| Ale (morning strength) | 1 copper |
| Hot cider | 2 copper |

---

Theron checked his pockets. In the chaos of escape, he still had his coin purse—**14 copper and 3 silver**. The witch wanted his blood, not his money.

*He slid five copper and one more across the bar.*

"Bacon, eggs, black bread. And an ale."

The barkeep nodded approvingly. "A proper breakfast. Find a seat—Marta will bring it out."

He chose a table near the hearth, positioning himself with his back to the wall and a clear view of the room. Whisper slipped from his hood and disappeared beneath the table, invisible to casual observers.

"The two in the corner," the familiar whispered from below. "They smell like steel and old blood. Professionals."

---

## The Hunters

The food arrived. Theron ate slowly, savoring every bite—the salt of the bacon, the richness of the yolk, the dark chew of the bread. The ale was weak but welcome, washing away the last chill of the river.

The meal restored him. The fog of exhaustion lifted.

---

As he ate, he observed.

**The cloaked figures:**

One is a woman—sharp-featured, silver hair pulled back tight, a scar running from her left temple to her jaw. She traces routes on the map with a gloved finger.

The other is broader, male, dark-skinned with a close-cropped beard. A heavy crossbow leans against the wall beside him. He listens more than he speaks.

Their conversation is low, but he caught fragments:

*"...Thornhollow's been active again..."*

*"...the bounty's good but the last crew never came back..."*

*"...need better intel before we move..."*

The woman glances up. Her eyes meet his for a half-second—assessing, calculating—then return to the map.

Whisper's voice floats up. "They're discussing *her*. The witch."

---

## Gathering Intelligence

Theron mopped up the last of the egg yolk with his bread, then rose casually and returned to the bar. The barkeep was drying tankards with a rag, watching her domain with the practiced awareness of someone who'd seen everything.

He leaned against the counter, keeping his voice low.

"Those two in the corner. They regulars?"

She glanced past him, then back. Her expression doesn't change, but something flickers behind her eyes—caution, maybe respect for his discretion.

"Regulars enough. They've been through Millhaven three, four times this season." She sets down a tankard. "Hunters. The dangerous kind. Not bandits or beasts—they hunt *worse* things."

"They any good?"

A dry laugh. "They're still alive, aren't they? The woman's called **Sera Vex**. Used to be a soldier—officer, I heard, before something went sour. The big fellow's **Tomren**. Doesn't talk much. Doesn't need to, with that crossbow of his."

She leans closer, voice dropping.

"Word is they've been poking around Thornhollow for weeks. Asking questions. Mapping the forest." Her eyes narrow. "You're the second person this month to stumble in here looking like they crawled out of a grave. The last one..."

She trails off.

"The last one what?"

"Didn't make it through the night. Fever took him. Kept mumbling about a witch and something she'd *summoned*."

Whisper shifts beneath his collar.

*The barkeep studies he with newfound interest.*

"You've got that same look, stranger. But you're still standing." She nods toward the corner. "If you know something about what's up in those woods—those two are your best bet. Just..."

She hesitates.

"Don't waste their time. Sera doesn't suffer fools."

---

## First Contact

Theron approached the table with unhurried steps, hands visible, posture open—no threat here, just a traveler. Sera's eyes tracked him the moment he left the bar. Tomren's hand drifted toward the crossbow, then stopped. Waiting.

"Apologies for the interruption," Theron said, voice calm. "I couldn't help but overhear you mention Thornhollow."

Sera's scarred face remains unreadable. "Lots of people mention Thornhollow. It's the local nightmare. Doesn't mean we're taking applications."

"I'm not applying. I'm offering."

A flicker of interest. Tomren glanced at his partner.

Theron continuedd, measured. "I was in those woods recently. Saw things. Learned things. The kind of information that might be worth something to people in your line of work."

Sera leaned back, arms crossed. "And what line of work would that be?"

"The kind where you're still alive after poking around a witch's territory for weeks."

*Silence. The fire crackles.*

Then—a ghost of a smile tugs at Sera's scarred lip.

"Sit down."

---

## Testing Motives

Theron held Sera's gaze. Trust was a bridge, and he wasn't crossing it alone.

"Before I answer that," he said slowly, "I'd like to understand what I'm walking into. The barkeep says you've been circling Thornhollow for weeks. Mapping. Asking questions." He paused. "That's not how someone chases a bounty. That's how someone plans a war."

Sera's expression flickered—surprise, then something harder. Respect, maybe. Or irritation at being read so easily.

Tomren shifted in his seat. His eyes cut to Sera, questioning.

*Silence stretches.*

Then Sera exhales, a long, controlled breath. She reaches up and tugs down the collar of her cloak, revealing a leather cord around her neck. Hanging from it: a small silver locket, tarnished with age.

"My daughter," she says quietly. "Elina. Sixteen years old. Wanted to be a healer."

*Wanted.*

"Six months ago, the witch took her. Snatched her right off the road between here and Dunmere. We found her horse. Her satchel. Nothing else."

Her jaw tightens.

"The bounty's real—local lord posted it after his own son vanished. But I'm not here for gold." She tucks the locket away. "I'm here to burn that castle to the ground and bring my daughter home. Alive, if the gods are kind. If not..."

She doesn't finish.

Tomren speaks, his gravelly voice soft.

"Sera saved my life. Twice. I go where she goes."

---

*There's no deception here. Only grief sharpened into purpose.*

This woman has spent six months preparing, planning, waiting for the right moment. She's not reckless—she's *patient*. And she's been searching for exactly what he offer: insider knowledge.

But there's something else beneath the surface. A flicker of fear she's trying to bury.

She's afraid her daughter is already dead. Or worse—already *changed*.

---

## The Alliance

Sera meets his eyes again.

"So now you know my stake, wizard." The word isn't an insult—she's identified your profession from Whisper, from your bearing, from the way you carry yourself without weapons but not without threat. "I'm not asking you to fight beside us out of charity. I'm asking because we need each other."

She leans forward.

"You know the castle. The tunnels. The defenses. You've *seen* what she summoned—and you're still sane enough to sit here and bargain." Her voice drops. "That makes you more valuable than every scout I've hired and lost."

Tomren grunts agreement.

"So I'll ask again." Sera's scarred face is intent, focused. "What do you want? And what will it take to make this partnership work?"

---

*Theron nod slowly, letting her words settle. She's offered him her wound. The least he can do is offer his.*

"The witch took something from me, too. Not a person—but part of who I am." You lay your hands flat on the table. "My spellbook. My component pouch. Years of study, research, spells I developed myself. She's wearing my pouch as a *trophy*."

Sera's eyes flicker with understanding. For a wizard, a spellbook isn't just a tool. It's a piece of his soul bound in leather and ink.

"Without them, I can manage cantrips. Minor tricks. But with them?" You let a hint of steel enter your voice. "I could be the difference between success and another team that never comes back."

Tomren leans forward, interested now.

"So we retrieve your belongings," Sera says. "Seems reasonable. What else?"

*Here's the line he need to draw.*

"Assurances," you say carefully. "If we find your daughter—and I hope we do—she becomes the priority. We get her out *first*. I understand that. I respect it."

Sera's expression tightens, but she's listening.

"But I need to know the same courtesy extends to me. If we reach my spellbook, if we find my components, I won't be told to leave them behind because the mission's getting hot. I won't be abandoned mid-operation because my objectives aren't *yours*."

Theron held her gaze.

"I've already been a prisoner once this week. I won't be a disposable asset."

---

*Silence falls over the table.*

Tomren looks to Sera. The fire crackles. Somewhere outside, a cart rattles past.

Then Sera extends her hand across the table.

"You have my word," she says. "Elina first—but your belongings are part of the mission, not an afterthought. We go in together, we come out together. All of us. All of our objectives."

Her grip is calloused, strong.

"Betray us, and Tomren puts a bolt through your spine. But keep faith?" A grim smile. "We'll burn that witch's world down and take back everything she stole."

**Insight—one final check.**

*She means it. There's no hidden clause, no mental reservation. Sera Vex keeps her promises—it's probably the only thing holding her together.*

---

Theron clasped her hand.

"Then we have an accord."

Tomren nods, something like approval in his dark eyes. Whisper chititters softly from his shoulder.

"Well," the familiar murmurs, "this should be *interesting*."

---

# Chapter 4: The Planning

Sera releases his hand and spreads the map back across the table.

"We've been gathering intelligence for weeks, but it's all external. Patrol routes. Supply lines. The castle's silhouette." She taps the parchment. "You're the first person to make it out of there alive and lucid. So."

She slides the map toward him.

"Show us everything. Entrances. Exits. Guard positions. Where she keeps prisoners. Where she works." Her eyes glint. "And tell us about this *summoning*. What came through?"

---

## The Layout

*Theron pull the map closer, studying its edges. Sera's cartography is meticulous—the castle's exterior, the treeline, the river he know all too well. But the interior is blank. Waiting.*

"Do you have charcoal? A quill?"

Tomren produces a stick of graphite from his pack. He took it and begin to sketch.

"The castle has three levels that I know of. Possibly more."

His hand moves across the parchment, roughing in shapes from memory.

"The dungeons are the lowest. Cells here, here, and here." You mark Xs. "Mine was the third on the left. Stone walls, iron bars, old but solid. There's a central corridor that runs the length of the dungeon, lit by torches that never seem to burn out."

"Enchanted?" Sera asks.

"Almost certainly."

Theron trace a line downward.

"Below the main dungeon level, there's a drainage system. Old tunnels—pre-dates the castle, I think. This is how I escaped." You mark the grate's position. "The ironwork was rusted. One of her thralls broke it down chasing an illusion."

A faint smirk from Tomren.

"The tunnels branch extensively. Without a guide, you'd be lost within minutes. Some passages are collapsed. At least one leads to an oubliette—Whisper warned me away from it."

The familiar speaks up from your shoulder. "Deep pit. Old bones at the bottom. She throws failures down there."

Sera's jaw tightens. She's thinking of Elina.

---

Theron continued, sketching upward now.

"Above the dungeons, a spiral staircase connects to what I believe is the main level. I didn't see it directly, but I heard the ritual coming from above. The acoustics suggest a large central chamber—great hall, perhaps, or a converted throne room."

"That's where she'd work," Whisper adds. "She likes theatrical spaces. Room for circles. Altars. Audiences."

"Audiences?" Tomren rumbles.

"She talks to *something*. Always has. Whether it listens..." The rat shrugs, disturbingly human. "Who knows?"

Theron marked the probable location of the ritual chamber.

"Her study is somewhere on this level as well. That's where my spellbook will be—she'd want to examine it, learn what I know. Witches covet other practitioners' secrets."

"And the component pouch?" Sera asks.

"On her person. She was wearing it when I heard her voice from above."

---

## The Defenses

Theron set down the graphite and lean back.

"Thralls. At least three, possibly more. Human once—or close enough. They move wrong. Jerky, like puppets with tangled strings. They don't speak, but they *track*. By scent, I think. Maybe sound."

"Can they be killed?" Tomren asks, hand resting on his crossbow.

"They're not undead, if that's what you're asking. They bleed. A bolt to the head would put one down, I'd wager." You pause. "But they're *fast* when they want to be. And they don't seem to feel pain."

Sera nods grimly. "We've dealt with enthralled before. Aim for the legs first, then finish them."

Theron continued.

"There's also her familiar network. Or there *was*." You glance at Whisper. "She keeps eyes and ears throughout the castle. Rats, ravens, spiders—small creatures bound to her will. They report back. If one sees you, she knows."

"Present company excluded," Whisper says drily.

"Can you identify the others?" Sera asks the rat directly.

"Some. Not all. She's had centuries to build her web." Whisper's whiskers twitch. "But I know which ones are truly loyal and which are merely... *bound*. There may be opportunities for misdirection."

Sera absorbs this. "What about magical defenses? Wards? Alarms?"

"The silver key," you say. "In the dungeon, there were three keys. One opened my cell. One—the silver one, with runes—triggers an alarm if used. I don't know what the bone-white key does, and I didn't ask."

"Smart," Tomren mutters.

"Assume wards on every significant entrance. The main doors, her study, the ritual chamber. I can't tell you specifics without examining them, but a witch of her power wouldn't leave herself exposed."

---

## The Summoning

The tavern feels colder suddenly. Even the fire seems to dim.

"I didn't see it directly," you admit. "I was in the tunnels when it happened. But I *felt* it."

Theron searched for words adequate to the memory.

"The chanting stopped. Then—a sound. Like reality being torn. Fabric ripping, but *deeper*. It resonated in my bones, my teeth." You shake your head. "And then her voice changed. Layered. As if something else was speaking *through* her. Or *with* her."

Sera's face has gone pale.

"What did it say?"

"'The binding is complete. Now... where is my sacrifice?'"

Silence.

Tomren's hand tightens on his crossbow.

"She's bound something," you continue. "Something powerful enough to require a sacrifice. Something that can speak through her." You look at Whisper. "Do you know what it might be?"

The familiar is quiet for a long moment.

"She's been researching for decades. Collecting texts. Obsessing over old names." His voice is unusually subdued. "There were three entities she spoke of most often. Three she believed could be bound, if the price was paid."

He meets his eyes.

"**Vaelthar the Unraveler**—a demon of entropy. It consumes magical bonds, unweaves enchantments. Dangerous to casters, but relatively containable."

"**Morreth, the Choir of Wounds**—not one being but many, fused together. It speaks with borrowed voices and feeds on pain. Harder to kill because there's no single point of failure."

"Or..."

Whisper hesitates.

"**Szeth-Kaan, the Herald of Empty Thrones.**"

Even the name feels wrong in his ears. Sera shivers.

"What is that?" she asks.

"Old. Very old. A thing from before the gods carved their names into the world. It doesn't want worship. It doesn't want souls." Whisper's voice drops to a whisper. "It wants *endings*. Kingdoms. Bloodlines. Legacies. It erases things from history so completely that no one remembers they existed."

The fire crackles.

"If she's bound Szeth-Kaan..." The familiar trails off. "Then the sacrifice isn't a person. It's a *place*. A village. A family line. Something with roots."

Sera's hand moves to the locket at her throat.

---

*The weight of the situation settles over the table like a shroud.*

Sera speaks first, her voice rough.

"Then we don't have weeks. We might not even have days." She looks at you. "Can you tell which one it is? From what you heard?"

*Theron closed his eyes, reaching back into the memory. The sound. The voice. The way the air tasted—ozone and old blood.*

"The layered voice," you say slowly. "It wasn't a chorus. It was singular, but *fractured*. Like one speaker echoing through broken mirrors."

Whisper nods. "That rules out Morreth. The Choir speaks in true multiplicity—dozens of voices at once, overlapping. What you heard was one entity, fragmented."

"And the feeling when it arrived. Not unraveling. Not dissolution." You press your palm against the table, remembering. "It felt like *erasure*. Like the space where the ritual happened was being... edited. Removed from the world's memory even as it occurred."

Sera's face has gone ashen.

"Szeth-Kaan," Whisper confirms, his voice barely audible. "The Herald of Empty Thrones."

---

*Silence hangs heavy.*

Tomren breaks it, his gravelly voice cutting through the dread.

"So we're not just fighting a witch. We're fighting a witch *and* an ancient god-thing that erases people from existence."

"Not a god," Whisper corrects. "Older than gods. Gods have egos, desires, worshippers. Szeth-Kaan is more like... a function. A cosmic editor. It removes things that have grown too *loud* in the weave of history."

"And the witch summoned it *why*?" Sera demands.
